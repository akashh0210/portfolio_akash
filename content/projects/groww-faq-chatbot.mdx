## Problem

Groww's mutual fund FAQ content is extensive but scattered. Users asking investment-specific questions get generic search results or irrelevant support articles. Support volume for answerable FAQ queries is high.

## Hypothesis

A RAG pipeline over the structured FAQ corpus would let users ask natural-language questions and get grounded, cited answers — reducing support escalations for FAQ-answerable queries.

## What I built

A Python ingestion pipeline chunked and embedded 2,916 FAQ entries into ChromaDB. A FastAPI endpoint retrieves the top-k relevant chunks and passes them with the query to Llama 3 for a grounded response. Every answer includes source attribution linking back to the original FAQ.

## Outcome & learning

2,916 chunks indexed, sub-3-second response latency, answers grounded in retrieved context. Key learning: chunk size matters more than model choice for RAG quality — smaller, semantically coherent chunks produced noticeably better retrieval precision than larger splits.
